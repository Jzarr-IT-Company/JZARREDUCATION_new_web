1. What is product sourcing in Amazon business?

Product sourcing is the process of finding suppliers or manufacturers to purchase products that will be sold on Amazon.

2. Why is product sourcing important?

Good sourcing ensures product quality, competitive pricing, and reliable supply for your business.

3. What will I learn in the product sourcing module?

Students learn how to find suppliers, negotiate pricing, and evaluate product quality.

4. Where do Amazon sellers find suppliers?

Many sellers find suppliers through platforms like Alibaba and AliExpress.

5. How do I evaluate a supplier?

Supplier evaluation includes checking reviews, communication quality, product samples, and reliability.

6. What is MOQ in product sourcing?

MOQ stands for Minimum Order Quantity, which is the smallest order size a supplier accepts.

7. Why are product samples important?

Samples help sellers verify product quality before placing large orders.

8. Can I negotiate prices with suppliers?

Yes, negotiation is common when ordering products in bulk.

9. What is private labeling in Amazon selling?

Private labeling means selling products under your own brand name.

10. What should I consider before choosing a supplier?

Factors include pricing, quality, shipping time, reliability, and communication.

11. How do sellers ensure product quality?

Quality control may include sample testing and supplier verification.

12. What shipping methods are used for sourcing?

Common methods include air shipping and sea freight depending on cost and urgency.

13. Can beginners learn product sourcing easily?

Yes, this module explains sourcing strategies step by step for beginners.

14. What risks exist in product sourcing?

Risks may include poor product quality, shipping delays, or supplier miscommunication.

15. What skills will I gain from this module?

Students learn supplier communication, negotiation, sourcing strategies, and supply chain basics.
